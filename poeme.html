THE END OF TEXAS RED

(Une ballade écrite par Adam Abdat)

In the state of New Mexico lived an outlaw, Texas red,
All the men who tried to take him ended dead,
But one day a stranger rode into town,
Didn’t say much and kept his head down.


He walked calmly into the saloon,
It was 7 minutes till high noon,
He spoke to the bartender, said he was a contender,
To try and make Texas Red surrender.


Texas Red heard word and challenged him to a duel,
As the ranger accepted, townsfolk thought he was a tool,
They agreed to draw at noon, everyone held their breath,
They thought this ranger was about to meet his death.


As the clock struck, a single shot was heard,
The speed at which the winner drew was absurd,
Texas Red had not cleared leather,
The town was brought together.


There lied the outlaw, outmatched and defeated,
A single shot to the head, he couldn’t be treated,
That day the ranger’s revolver,
Was the town’s problem solver.



(Ce poème suit l'histoire d'une chanson au même nom, mais sous forme d'une balade. Une forme de poésie centrée sur la narration d'une histoire et des rimes.)
